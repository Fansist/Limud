# Congressional App Challenge 2026 — Requirements Checklist

> **What this is:** every rule and requirement you must satisfy to enter the 2026 Congressional App Challenge (CAC). Requirements only — nothing about any specific app. Each checklist item has a **footnote** with the exact official wording and where it comes from, so you can see the full detail behind every box.
>
> **Source of truth:** the official **2026 CAC Rules** PDF and rules pages (verified July 2026). If anything here ever conflicts with the official site, the official site wins.[^src] The Administrators (Internet Education Foundation) can modify the competition or judging at any time.[^modify]

---

## ⏱️ Key dates (2026 cycle)

| Date | What |
|---|---|
| Jan 1, 2026 | Pre-registration opens[^dates] |
| May 2026 | Challenge launches; submissions open[^deadline] |
| **Mon, Oct 26, 2026 — 12:00 PM EDT** | **Submission deadline (hard).** After this, the submission cannot be modified in any way.[^deadline][^nomod] |
| After deadline | Exit Questionnaire sent (required)[^exit] |
| Nov 2026 | Judging by each Member of Congress / their office |
| By Jan 15, 2027 | Winners announced (embargo until then)[^embargo] |

## 1. Eligibility — you must be able to check ALL of these
- [ ] Each participant is enrolled in **middle school or high school on Oct 26, 2026**.[^age]
- [ ] You are entering in a **Congressional district that is hosting** a Challenge — either where you **live** or where you **attend school**.[^location]
- [ ] You are submitting in **only one district** (no multi-district entries).[^location]
- [ ] **One app per person per year** — you are not part of any other entry/team this year.[^oneentry]
- [ ] Team size is **1–4 students**; if a team, **at least half** of the members live or attend school in the **same** district.[^teams]
- [ ] The app was **created after Oct 30, 2025**. (For a "2.0" of an older app, only the **new** code is judged.)[^prior]
- [ ] All participants are **U.S. residents** at submission (no citizenship requirement).[^residency]

## 2. Registration — you must provide, per participant
- [ ] A **personal (non-school) email address**.[^reg]
- [ ] **Home address** with **9-digit ZIP**, Congressional District, and Member of Congress.[^reg]
- [ ] **School address** with **9-digit ZIP**, Congressional District, and Member of Congress.[^reg]
- [ ] A **parent/guardian's** name and email.[^reg]
- [ ] *(Optional)* coding teacher/mentor name and email.[^reg]
- [ ] Register online at **congressionalappchallenge.us** and pass the **eligibility quiz**. Teams: one person creates the profile and invites up to 3 others.[^regprocess]

## 3. The app itself — it must satisfy ALL of these
- [ ] **Functionality:** the app actually works to some meaningful degree (judges can demand to see it run).[^function]
- [ ] **Originality:** original and created solely by the student(s); **all coding/technical development done by you**. No third party holds any rights or interest.[^original]
- [ ] **Open-source use is allowed but must be clearly documented**, and the project must reflect significant personal effort + technical understanding.[^original]
- [ ] **AI use is allowed ONLY IF:** it is **fully disclosed** in the submission, it **only supports specific parts** of the project (not the whole build), and you can **demonstrate significant individual contribution and technical understanding**.[^ai]
- [ ] **No IP / privacy violations:** must not violate any intellectual-property, common-law, or privacy rights of third parties.[^content]
- [ ] **Content decency:** not indecent, defamatory, hateful, threatening, or in bad taste; no pornography/obscenity; nothing that harms a district's reputation or incites violence.[^content]
- [ ] *(Any topic, any platform, any programming language is fine.)*[^platform]

## 4. The demonstration video — the single most important piece
- [ ] **Length 1–3 minutes** (max 3:00). Going over/under may be penalized at judges' discretion.[^vidlen]
- [ ] Hosted on **YouTube or Vimeo**, set to **PUBLIC** (not unlisted/private).[^vidhost]
- [ ] States the **name(s) of each participant**.[^vidcontent]
- [ ] States the **name of the app**.[^vidcontent]
- [ ] States the **purpose of the app in one clear sentence**.[^vidcontent]
- [ ] States the **target audience** (who it's for).[^vidcontent]
- [ ] States the **tools and programming languages** used.[^vidcontent]
- [ ] **Showcases the app's functionality** (shows it working).[^vidcontent]

## 5. Written questions — you must answer all six
- [ ] 1. Title of your app.[^questions]
- [ ] 2. The app's purpose.[^questions]
- [ ] 3. What inspired you to create it.[^questions]
- [ ] 4. The technical/coding difficulty you faced and **how you solved it**.[^questions]
- [ ] 5. What you learned / your biggest takeaway from the CAC.[^questions]
- [ ] 6. What you'd change in a **2.0** version.[^questions]

## 6. Submitting & after
- [ ] Complete the online submission (video link + written answers) **before the deadline**; it locks at 12:00 PM EDT Oct 26, 2026.[^deadline][^nomod]
- [ ] Keep the **app and source code available** — judges may request access in person or otherwise to verify it works; **refusing = immediate disqualification**.[^verify]
- [ ] After the deadline, **every team member individually** completes the **Exit Questionnaire**.[^exit]
- [ ] Understand you're agreeing to the official rules, a publicity/likeness release, and a royalty-free license for CAC to post your submission (you keep IP ownership).[^release]

## 7. How you're judged (know what wins)
Evaluated by the Member of Congress / their office (they may use a judging panel), on **three official criteria**:[^judging]
- [ ] **Quality of the idea** — including creativity and originality.[^judging]
- [ ] **Implementation of the idea** — including user experience and design.[^judging]
- [ ] **Demonstrated excellence of coding and programming skills.**[^judging]

> ⚠️ **Rubric note:** The official rules list the three criteria above (no point values). A separate **"sample judging rubric" PDF** is linked from the CAC site and some districts use it — treat it as guidance, not the binding rule.[^judging]

## Footnotes — full official wording & references

[^src]: Source: official **2026 CAC Rules** PDF, `congressionalappchallenge.us/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/2026-CAC-Rules.pdf`, plus the Rules and Student Registration pages at `congressionalappchallenge.us/students/`. Administrator: the **Internet Education Foundation**, Washington, DC. Verified July 2026.

[^modify]: Rules §12 & §7.4: "Administrators reserve the right to cancel, suspend and/or modify the Competition… Members of Congress and their staff reserve the right to substitute or modify the judging process or criteria at any time for any reason." Always re-check the official site before submitting.

[^dates]: Season timeline from the CAC rules pages: pre-registration opens January 1; the Challenge launches in May; judging in November; winners in December/announced by the hosting office.

[^deadline]: Rules §5 "Deadline": "The Congressional App Challenge will accept submissions from May 2026 – October 26th: **12:00 pm EDT, Monday, October 26th, 2026.**"

[^nomod]: Rules §4 "Online Submission": "After the Submission Period has ended, the Submission cannot be modified in any way."

[^exit]: Rules §6 "Exit Questionnaire": "Once the deadline passes, students will receive a link to the Exit Questionnaire… **ALL MEMBERS of your team must complete the Exit Questionnaire individually.** Teammates can use the same link, but must complete the form individually."

[^embargo]: Rules §8 "Winner Announcements": announcements are made by the hosting Member of Congress; the CAC team "cannot announce or disclose the results… prior to the embargo date of **January 15, 2027.**"

[^age]: Rules §1 "AGE": "Students must be enrolled in middle school or high school on **October 26th, 2026.** Middle school varies by region but typically encompasses 6th-8th grades."

[^location]: Rules §1 "LOCATION": "Students are only allowed to compete in a Congressional District which is hosting an App Challenge. Students may compete in either the district in which they reside, or the district in which they attend school. Participants are only allowed to submit an app in **one district.** They cannot compete in multiple districts." (Check your district is participating: `congressionalappchallenge.us/students/participating-districts/`.)

[^oneentry]: Rules §1 "ONE ENTRY PER PERSON PER YEAR": "Students may only submit **ONE app per year.** Multiple entries across multiple teams is not allowed."

[^teams]: Rules §1 "TEAMS": "Students can compete as individuals, or in teams of up to **four (4) students.** Any team with more than four (4) students is not eligible… **At least half of teammates** on any team must live or attend school in the same Congressional District."

[^prior]: Rules §1 "PRIOR PROJECTS": "Students may submit any app they've created **after October 30th, 2025.** If submitting a 2.0 version of an app, only the new coding should be highlighted for consideration."

[^residency]: Eligibility (rules pages): all competing students must be **U.S. residents** at the time of submission; "There is no citizen requirement."

[^reg]: Rules §2 "Online Registration" — to register as an individual you need: a personal email that is **not** your school email; home address incl. **9-digit zip code**, Congressional District, and Member of Congress; school address incl. 9-digit zip, District, and Member of Congress; a parent/guardian's name and email; (optional) coding teacher/mentor name and email. Teams need the above **for each member**.

[^regprocess]: Rules §4: register at `congressionalappchallenge.us`; complete a personal profile and **eligibility quiz**; if eligible you're emailed to proceed. For teams, "one teammate should create the team profile, which will allow that user to invite up to three other team members to edit the application."

[^function]: Rules §3 "FUNCTIONALITY": "The app must demonstrate some degree of functionality to be considered competitive."

[^original]: Rules §3 "ORIGINALITY": "The app must be original and solely created by the contestant. **All coding and technical development must be done by the student or student team.** While participants may use open-source libraries, frameworks, and external tools, they must **clearly document any such usage** and ensure their project reflects significant personal effort and technical understanding. No other party should have any rights or interest in the app…"

[^ai]: Rules §3 "AI USAGE": "The use of AI tools in app development… is **permitted, provided that all AI usage is fully disclosed** in the submission materials. AI may only be used to support **specific aspects** of the project and **must not constitute the entirety of the technical development.** Participants are expected to demonstrate significant individual contributions and technical understanding of their app."

[^content]: Rules §3 "CONTENT SUITABILITY": must adhere to "standards of decency and respect… must not be indecent, defamatory, in obvious bad taste… must not adversely affect the reputation of Congressional districts, depict hatred, defame, or threaten any specific community, or incite violence. Apps containing pornography, obscenity, or sexual activity are strictly prohibited. Additionally, the app **must not violate any Intellectual Property, common law, or privacy rights of third parties.**"

[^platform]: Rules §3: TOPIC — any topic; PLATFORM — any (web, desktop, mobile, browser extension, robot, AR/VR, wearable, smart/embedded, etc.); PROGRAMMING LANGUAGE — any (C, C++, Objective-C, C#, Java, JavaScript, Python, Ruby, PHP, Swift, block-based, etc.).

[^vidlen]: Rules §4 "DEMONSTRATION VIDEO (max 3 min)": "The video should be **1-3 minutes long.** Video submissions which do not adhere to the time constraints may be penalized by the judges at their discretion. **This is not a video-creation competition.** However, the judges view the demonstration video to learn about the app."

[^vidhost]: Rules §4: "Upload the completed video to **YouTube or Vimeo.** The video must be set to **'public'.**"

[^vidcontent]: Rules §4: the video must include "**The name(s) of each participant; The name of the app; Clearly explain the purpose of the app** (…in one, clear sentence); **Explain the app's target audience** (who the app is intended for?); **The tools and coding languages used** to create the app; **Showcase the functionality of the app.**"

[^questions]: Rules §4 "QUESTIONS": "the team will need to answer a series of questions similar to the following: 1. What is the title of your app? 2. Explain the app's purpose. 3. What inspired you to create this app? 4. What technical/coding difficulty did you face in programming your app, and how did you address this technical challenge? 5. What did you learn while participating in the CAC? What was your biggest takeaway? 6. What would you change about your app if you were to create a 2.0 version?"

[^verify]: Rules §7.3 "Winner Selection": "The Judges have the right to request access to the App and source code in person or via any reasonable manner to verify that the App functions and operates as stated in the Submission Form. **Failure by a Contestant to honor such a request will result in the Submission's immediate disqualification.**"

[^release]: Rules §10 "Entry Conditions and Release" & §11 "Publicity and Intellectual Property": entrants agree to be bound by the official rules; consent to use of name/likeness/voice for promotion; "Contestants will **own the intellectual property rights** to their Submission, but will grant to Congress and the Administrator a **royalty-free license** to post the submission online and use it for noncommercial purposes."

[^judging]: Rules §7 "Winner Selection": "Demonstration Videos and Submission Answers will be reviewed and evaluated by the Member of Congress and their office. Members of Congress may opt to use a panel of judges… Apps can be judged based on the following criteria: **a. Quality of the idea (including creativity and originality); b. Implementation of the idea (including user experience and design); c. Demonstrated excellence of coding and programming skills.**" A separate sample-rubric PDF is linked from the CAC site as optional guidance.
